SEO vs GEO

What is the difference between SEO and GEO?

Traditional SEO optimizes to rank your page in a list of blue links a person clicks. GEO — Generative Engine Optimization, and its cousin AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) — optimizes to make your business the source the AI summarizes and cites inside the answer itself, often with no click at all. With roughly 60% of Google searches now ending without a click, being named in the answer matters as much as ranking in the list.

Traditional SEO GEO / AEO
Goal Rank high in a list of links Be the source named & cited in the AI answer
Where you win Page-one results ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity
Success metric Clicks & organic traffic Citations & mentions in generated answers
Primary signals Backlinks, keywords, page speed Schema, complete Google Business Profile, recent reviews, Reddit & forum mentions
Content style Keyword-targeted pages Direct, quotable answers to real questions
Result of winning A visitor to your site A recommendation made on your behalf

Is GEO replacing SEO?

No — it's layered on top. The same fundamentals (a fast, crawlable site, accurate listings, real reviews) still matter. GEO adds the structured data and quotable content that let an answer engine repeat your business confidently instead of guessing or skipping you.

Why does my SEO ranking not get me cited?

Ranking proves a page is relevant; it doesn't hand the AI a clean, structured fact to quote. Without schema markup, a complete Google Business Profile, and direct question-answer content, the engine has nothing easy to lift — so it cites a competitor or a Reddit thread instead.
